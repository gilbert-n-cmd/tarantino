The Parent-Teacher Association (PTA) at Bishop Angelo Tarantino Memorial Secondary School plays a crucial role in strengthening the partnership between the school administration, teachers, and parents. Through active involvement, the PTA ensures that learners receive holistic education in a safe, supportive, and well-resourced environment.

What is the PTA Executive Committee?

The PTA Executive Committee is a democratically elected body of parents and staff members entrusted with representing the voices of the wider parent community. They work alongside school leadership to improve learning facilities, student welfare, discipline, and school development projects.

Why the PTA Matters

The PTA Executive helps bridge the gap between home and school. Their leadership encourages dialogue, supports policy implementation, and mobilizes resources. A strong PTA ensures transparency, accountability, and collaboration that benefits every learner.

How You Can Get Involved

Every parent is a valued member of the PTA and is encouraged to participate actively. Whether through attending meetings, volunteering, or contributing ideas, your involvement helps build a better future for our learners. Elections for the executive are held every two years, and any active parent is eligible to stand for office.

Conclusion

At Bishop Angelo Tarantino Memorial Secondary School, we believe that when parents and teachers work together, children thrive. The PTA Executive is committed to ensuring that our school remains a center of academic excellence, moral integrity, and community development. Together, we can make a lasting impact.
